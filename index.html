About

I am an independent researcher working as a sole author across molecular phylogenetics, linguistic typology, grammatical formalization, historical musicology, and philosophy of science. The programme spans twenty-three papers, all in progress, and is designed to be carried out over a lifetime.

The programme has a single deep motivation: evolution. Biological structures change, languages drift, musical traditions transform — and yet comparison across these changes is both indispensable and philosophically fraught. What legitimizes a claim that two proteins share a fold, that two grammatical features are the same, that a countertenor tradition continues a castrato one? The recurring question is what persists through change, and under what conditions persistence licenses comparison.

The answers I am pursuing are structural rather than historical. Persistently Reproducible Modules formalize what evolution reliably reconstructs across independent lineages; typed feature structures make the basis of grammatical comparison explicit; measurement invariance testing asks whether typological databases actually measure the same thing across macroareas. Each is a different angle on the same problem.

Outside research I sing as an amateur countertenor.
