Research

I
Molecular phylogenetics

Evolutionary fate of the Sm/Lsm/Hfq fold across the three domains of life.

+

The Sm/Lsm/Hfq superfamily provides a natural experiment in the fate of a protein fold under extreme genome reduction. An integrated study (bio-c) combines a rooted dual-method phylogeny of bacterial Hfq, a three-domain census showing DPANN/Asgard archaea retain the Sm fold universally while the bacterial CPR radiation has lost it entirely, and a comparative analysis of Hfq loss across 71 genomes spanning eight lifestyles — revealing two distinct loss modes: reduction-associated (endosymbionts, mollicutes) and size-independent lineage loss (Actinobacteria, CPR radiation). A follow-on computational study (bio-e) uses E-Cell4 reaction-kinetics modelling and ancestral sequence reconstruction to trace when and where Hfq's active-cycling machinery was built up or eroded across the bacterial tree.

II
Linguistic typology

Quantitative reanalysis of Grambank, WALS, and AUTOTYP.

+

Three papers reanalyse the major cross-linguistic databases using modern statistical methods. The first (lang-a1) characterises the global structure of grammatical diversity using NeighborNet, community detection, and variation partitioning — quantifying how much is genealogical versus areal. The second (lang-a2) estimates per-feature vertical stability and horizontal diffusibility via Bayesian autologistic modelling. The third (lang-a3) applies item response theory and differential item functioning to test measurement invariance: 74.8% of Grambank features show DIF across macroareas, calling into question direct cross-linguistic comparisons. The comparability problem raised here supplies the typological motivation for the formalization programme (Domain III) and connects to the philosophy-of-measurement work (Domain V).

lang-a1 · lang-a2 · lang-a3 → github.com/Carlo-Broschi
III
Grammatical formalization

Typed feature structures and unification, motivated by the typological findings of Domain II.

+

A long-term programme formalizing neglected problems in case grammar and construction grammar within typed feature structures and unification. Cross-linguistic stability of the subject selection rule (lang-b1); redesign of Fillmore's Modality node as a radial operator category with a companion Grammar Matrix library (lang-b2a/b); intellectual history of case grammar as a research programme (lang-c); switch-reference formalization in Construction Grammar (lang-d); formal justification of RRG's Operator Projection for conditional typology (lang-e). Verification uses the DELPH-IN ecosystem (Grammar Matrix · ACE · LKB-FOS) and independently ALE on SWI-Prolog — a triangulation approach grounded in the same cross-linguistic data as Domain II.

lang-b1 · lang-b2a · lang-b2b · lang-c · lang-d · lang-e → github.com/Carlo-Broschi
IV
Historical musicology

The castrato–countertenor transition as a two-axis historical argument.

+

A two-paper study separating two claims the field has conflated. Vocal mechanism (castrato phonation cannot be reproduced by falsetto) is discontinuous; repertoire and institutional function are continuous through a centuries-long chain — travesti contralto, English cathedral falsetto tradition, Alfred Deller. Deller is the hinge: both a symptom of the cathedral supply and the cause of a new solo function. The second paper examines contemporary performance practice through discography and archival sources, analysing how modern countertenors engage with castrato repertoire without claiming to restore it.

music-a · music-b → github.com/Carlo-Broschi
V
Philosophy of science

Persistently Reproducible Modules and the problem of comparability across special sciences.

+

A programme organised around Persistently Reproducible Modules (PRM) — biological structures whose organisation is reproducible across phylogenetically independent lineages, formalizing the intuition behind de Duve's "frozen accidents." Papers address: PRM at the molecular level applied to protein structure (phd-a); a scale-independent hierarchical PRM framework (phd-b); de Duve's typology of biological singularity reconstructed philosophically and tested through PRM (phil-b/c); and the cross-domain problem of comparability and reference in biological and linguistic systematics (phil-a/stat-a), connecting back to the measurement-invariance findings of Domain II.
